Destination: Expand Your Horizon in the Pacific Northwest

View from the Tacoma Convention Center

Welcome to the stunning Puget Sound Region! Centered in Tacoma, Washington - the City of Destiny - Puget Sound Express puts you at the heart of the Pacific Northwest's most scenic and historic railroad area. Expand Your Horizon from the snow-capped peak of Mount Rainier to the bustling ports and railroads of Puget Sound, this region offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich railroad heritage, with pioneering air and maritime history.

Railroad Heritage

The Puget Sound region played a pivotal role in railroad history, with major railroads connecting its ports to the rest of the continent and extensive logging railroads. Today, you can explore this legacy at the Washington State History Museum's model railroad exhibit, Tacoma's restored Union Station, the Northwest Railway Museum, and scenic railroads like the Mount Rainier Railroad. Watch active freight yards of Class I railroads and shortlines like Tacoma Rail.

Regional Attractions

The Puget Sound region offers endless exploration opportunities. Mount Rainier National Park and Mount St. Helens National Monument provide spectacular hiking, scenic drives, and fascinating volcanic history. Visit the Museum of Glass in Tacoma, take a ferry ride on the Puget Sound, explore Seattle's Pike Place Market, or venture to Olympic National Park's rainforests. The region's air and nautical heritage is showcased in museums in Seattle, Tacoma, Olympia, and Bremerton including the US Naval Undersea Museum.

Getting Around

Tacoma sits at the heart of the Puget Sound region, just 30 minutes from Seattle-Tacoma International Airport. Amtrak serves the region with Cascades (Vancouver, BC to Eugene, OR), Coast Starlight (Seattle to Los Angeles) and Empire Builder (Seattle/Portland to Chicago). The modern Amtrak Tacoma Station connects with Sounder commuter trains and light rail to the convention center's downtown location with easy access to restaurants, hotels, and waterfront attractions.

Extend Your Stay: Expand Your Horizon with Alaska Cruises

Consider extending your Pacific Northwest adventure with an Alaska cruise! The Port of Seattle serves as a major departure point for Alaska cruises during the summer season. Several cruise lines including Princess, Holland America, Norwegian, Cunard, and Royal Caribbean offer 7-14 day voyages to Alaska's Inside Passage, featuring glaciers, wildlife, and scenic ports. Many cruises depart both before and after the convention, making it a perfect add-on to your trip.
